Thanksgiving in other countries

Country Name Date Activity
Canada Canadian Thanksgiving On the 2nd Monday in Oct. People celebrate the harvest and other blessings of the past year.
China “Chung Chiu” Moon Festival On the 15th day of the 8th lunar cycle Family and friends share mooncakes with one another, signifying unity and peace for the coming season.
Germany Erntedankfest ("Harvest Thanksgiving Festival") In Sep. or Oct., depending on the region It mostly celebrated by rural, religious groups and church in larger cities. Austria and Switzerland also take part in the festivities.
Japan Labor Thanksgiving Day On the 23rd of Nov. Nagano Labour Festival and Nagano Ebisuko Fireworks Festival.
Israel Sukkot, or the Feast of the Tabernacles On the 15th day of the Hebrew month of Tishrei It lasts for seven days and most for religion.
Brazil Dia de Ação de Graças On the last Thursday in November Many festivities were inspired by American traditions.
Malaysia Sukkot, or the Feast of the Tabernacles On the 15th day of the Hebrew month of Tishrei It’s celebrated by the native ethnic Kadazan-Dusuns people for the month of May. Rice and rice wines are mainly served. The closing Humabot ceremony is the liveliest ceremony filled with traditional dance and song performances.
United Kingdom United Kingdom Harvest Festival On the 22nd or 23rd in Sep. The celebrations usually include singing hymns, praying, and decorating churches with baskets of fruit and food.
